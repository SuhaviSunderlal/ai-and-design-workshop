# Part 3 — The Build: One Brand, Five Interns (speaking script)

**Lead:** Suhav · **Co-driver on demos:** Aditya · **Time budget:** ~35 min (target 30–40) · **Slides:** Part 3 divider → live tools → closing "same brand, five interns" slide
**Goal of this part:** everyone builds a tiny **Brand Kit** by hand (a moodboard), then watches that *same kit* get fed into five different free AI tools and come back consistent every time. The lesson lands physically: **a good brief travels.**
**Energy:** maker energy. Less "physics lesson," more "watch this." Keep demos crisp — momentum is the whole game. You are the creative director narrating; the tools are the interns.

### The one idea this part proves
> "We give *one* brand to *five* different interns — and they all stay on-message. Not because the tools are smart, but because we hand each of them the same kit." 

This is the live payoff of two Part 1 threads: **"show, don't tell"** (slide 8 — paste the moodboard instead of describing it) and **"give it a permanent style guide"** (slide 10, card 08 — system prompt). Say "remember the moodboard slide?" out loud when you paste it the first time.

## ▸ Divider + Frame — "One brand, five interns" *(~2 min)*

**On screen:** Part 03 divider · "The Build: One Brand, Five Interns" · *Hand the same kit to every tool and watch it stay on-message.*

**Say:**
> "Right — enough about how the intern thinks. Let's put it to work. Here's the game for the next half hour: we're going to invent *one* little brand, and then hand it to **five completely different AI interns** — one makes images, one makes a poster, one writes a brochure, one builds a live website, one builds a slide deck. And the whole time I want you watching for one thing: **do they stay on-brand?**
>
> The secret is a thing I'll call your **Brand Kit** — it's tiny. A moodboard image, three words, a colour palette, one sentence about who you are. That's it. We hand that same kit to every tool. Remember the 'show, don't tell' slide — pasting a picture instead of describing it? And the 'permanent style guide' idea? This is both of those, live, five times in a row.
>
> You're going to build your *own* kit in the next eight minutes. Then I drive the tools, you watch — and steal everything."

**Do:** Keep it to 90 seconds. Don't explain the tools yet — name the five outputs (images, post, brochure, website, deck) so they know the shape, then get them building. The energy shift here is "we're making things now" — sell that.

## ▸ Step 0 — Moodboard (the Brand Kit) — HANDS-ON *(~8 min)*

**This is the only big build-it-yourself block. Protect the time.** Everyone makes a one-page moodboard in Canva that becomes their brief for everything after.

**On screen:** your finished *Gut Sense* moodboard (show the destination first, ~45 sec), then switch to a blank Canva board so they can follow.

**Say (demo your board for 45 sec, then set them loose):**
> "Here's mine — *Gut Sense*. Look what's on it: four or five reference images for the vibe, a strip of colours, three words — *calm, clinical-warm, botanical* — and one sentence: 'evidence-based gut-health guidance for overwhelmed adults.' That whole board is my brief. Now you build yours. Eight minutes. Don't overthink it — pick a *feeling*, not a logo."

**Do — walk them through it fast, then circulate:**
1. In Canva, search the **"Mood board"** template (or a blank Whiteboard/Doc). 
2. **3 words** — the vibe (e.g. *bold, playful, retro* or *calm, premium, minimal*).
3. **A palette** — drag 3–4 colour squares, or upload one photo you love and Canva pulls its colours.
4. **4–6 reference images** — Canva's free photos, or drag in your own. Looks/textures/scenes, *not* logos.
5. **One sentence** — "I help ___ do ___, and it should feel ___."
6. **Export it as one image** (Share → Download → PNG) — *this single image is what we paste into every tool.*

**🎯 Interactive — "pick a feeling, shout it" (~30 sec, to start them):**
> "Before you touch the laptop — say your three words in your head. Anyone want to shout theirs?" [take 3–4: "warm, handmade, earthy"… "neon, loud, fun"…]
> "Perfect. That's the hardest part done. Now go find pictures that *feel* like those words."

**Facilitator moves while they build:**
- Circulate. The stuck ones are usually trying to design a *logo* — redirect: "no logos, just the *vibe*. Five photos that feel right."
- 2-minute and 1-minute warnings. It's fine if boards are rough — "rough is fine, it's a brief not a final."
- Tell them: **keep this tab open** — you'll paste this board into the tools you're about to see.

## ▸ Step 1 — Your logo, the "Reference → JSON → restyle" way (Google AI Studio) *(~6 min)*

**The star demo — and there's one technique to teach here, the whole slide: Reference → JSON → restyle.** You don't describe a style in a paragraph and you don't gamble on a lucky generation. You hand the model a *look* you love, have it write that look down as a reusable **JSON spec**, then keep the style and swap in *your* subject. We use it to make the brand's **logo** — then, from the *same spec*, a hero image, so two different assets come out unmistakably one brand.

**On screen:** Google AI Studio (aistudio.google.com), Gemini 2.5 Flash Image ("Nano Banana"). Have **one reference image** ready — a mark/illustration whose *style* you love (clean, minimal, botanical for Gut Sense). Your moodboard works as the reference too.

**Say (walk the three steps out loud — they're on the slide):**
> "First intern: the image-maker — and I'm going to show you the single most useful trick in the whole morning. Three steps. **One — reference.** I find an image whose *look* I love. Not to copy it — to borrow its *style*. **Two — extract the JSON.** I paste it in and say: *'Describe this image as a JSON prompt I can reuse — style, shapes, line weight, palette.'* [generate] Look at that — it writes the whole look down as fields I can edit. That's 'show, don't tell' from this morning, paying off: it can *see* the reference and translate it.
>
> **Three — restyle.** I keep every style field and change *only* the subject: *'Same style — now the subject is a simple leaf-monogram logo for Gut Sense, a calm gut-health brand, sage and cream.'* [generate] And because it's an image model — same prompt, never the same picture — I run it three or four times and pick. The intern hands me drafts; *I* bring the taste."

**Do:**
1. **Reference** — drop in one image whose style you want for the mark (a logo/illustration you admire, or your moodboard). *Find an image whose look you love.*
2. **Extract JSON** — ask the multimodal model: *"Describe this as a JSON image prompt I can reuse"* — `style`, `palette`, `shapes`, `line weight`, `mood`. Show the JSON on screen; that's the reusable spec.
3. **Restyle → the logo** — keep the style fields, replace only `subject` with your logo concept ("a minimal leaf-monogram for Gut Sense"). Generate a few (callback: Part 1, "run a few, pick one").
4. **Tweak one field, regenerate** — change *just* `palette` or `line weight`. This is the money moment: the room watches you *edit a spec*, not re-gamble.
5. **Restyle again → a hero image** — same JSON spec, swap `subject` to "a calm hero image, hands holding a mug." Two different assets, one obvious brand. "*That's* the spec travelling."
6. Pick the winners and **download them** — the logo mark + the hero image become the brand assets you reuse in steps 2–5.

**Honest caveat (say it — it's true and it's on-brand for this workshop):**
> "Two honesty notes on AI logos. One — don't ask it to bake *text* into the logo; image models mangle letters. Generate the *mark*, then set your brand name in Canva with a real font. Two — this is a raster concept, not a vector, and an AI-only mark isn't yours to trademark until you redraw and refine it. So it's AI for the *idea*, Canva for the *finish*. That's the pro move, not a workaround."

**🎯 Interactive — "vote on the three" (~20 sec):**
> "Which of these three marks is most *Gut Sense*? One… two… three." [count] "Split room — exactly like the sunsets this morning. No correct logo, just the one that fits. I pick the winner, *then* refine it in Canva."

**Say (the tool verdict — say it plainly, it's reassuring):**
> "This is Google AI Studio — completely free, no credit card. And the trick you just saw — reference, JSON, restyle — works in *any* multimodal tool: ChatGPT, Gemini. It's how you get *your* look on demand instead of describing it into a paragraph and hoping. Use Ideogram if you ever need real text *inside* an image."

## ▸ Step 3 — Brochure (NotebookLM → Canva) *(~4 min)*

**The point of this step:** keep the *words* on-brand, not just the look. NotebookLM is your brand-brain — it can only answer from what you feed it, so it can't drift off-message.

**On screen:** NotebookLM (notebooklm.google.com) with a new notebook; then Canva with a trifold template.

**Say:**
> "Third intern writes the brochure — but I don't want it inventing a brand voice. So I use NotebookLM. I paste my Brand Kit in as its *only source* — [add source: the one-liner, 3 words, palette, who it's for] — and now it physically can't go off-brand, because that's all it's allowed to read.
>
> Then I ask: *'Write trifold brochure copy — a cover tagline, three inside panels (the problem, my approach, what I offer), and a back panel — grounded only in these notes, in this voice.'* [generate] Look — every word is on-message, because it had nowhere else to go."

**Do:**
1. NotebookLM → **New notebook** → **Add source** → paste the Brand Kit text (one-liner + 3 words + audience + palette names).
2. In the chat, ask for the trifold copy (cover / problem / approach / offer / contact), "grounded only in these notes."
3. Copy the output into a **Canva brochure / trifold template**, drop in the AI image and palette. "Look stays consistent, words stay consistent."

**Say (tool verdict):**
> "NotebookLM's superpower is that it *only* knows what you give it — which is exactly why it's safe for brand copy. We saw this morning that AI makes things up confidently; this is one way to *fence it in*."

**🎯 Easter egg — hold this for the very end / gallery (do NOT spend time on it now):**
> NotebookLM can turn those same brand notes into a 2-minute **Audio Overview** — two AI hosts chatting about *your* brand like a mini-podcast (there's a video version too). Don't run it live here — it takes a minute to generate. Kick one off quietly, and play 20 seconds at the gallery walk as the "okay that's genuinely wild" closer.

## Facilitator notes
- **The moodboard is the only thing that *must* go well.** It's their hands-on artifact and the brief for everything else. Protect its 8 minutes; if you're behind, steal time from a demo, never from the moodboard.
- **Use ONE brand for all five demos.** This is non-negotiable for the lesson. Prepare the *Gut Sense* kit (or your own) fully *before* the session — moodboard image saved, palette hexes handy, one-liner memorised. Improvising a new brand per tool kills the whole point.
- **Pre-load every tab logged in.** A demo that opens on a Google sign-in screen kills momentum. Tabs open, accounts in, moodboard image downloaded and ready to drag.
- **If you're tight on time (likely — five demos in ~32 min is aggressive):** the two most droppable to a *30-second flash* are **brochure** (Step 3) and **presentation** (Step 5) — show the magic, skip the polish. **Never cut:** the moodboard (it's theirs) and the live website URL (Step 4 — it's the emotional peak).
- **If WiFi is shaky:** image generation and Gamma are the most bandwidth-hungry. Have one **pre-baked** result of each saved locally as a fallback so a demo never dies on a spinner — "here's one I made earlier."
- **The phone moment (Step 4) is the screenshot people take home.** Don't rush it. Open the live URL, hold the phone up, *be quiet for three seconds*, let them feel it.
- **Repeated line, every single step:** "same kit." Say it as you paste the moodboard into each tool. Repetition is what turns five demos into one lesson.
- **Free-tier honesty is on-brand for this workshop.** When you hit a paywall (Canva resize, Gamma credits), name it cheerfully and show the free lane. Our whole promise is "no friction, everyone can" — owning the limits *builds* trust.
- **Easter egg discipline:** the NotebookLM audio/video overview is a closer, not a step. Start it generating during Step 5, play 20 seconds at the gallery. One clean "whoa," not a sidetrack.
