Why we do it this way

Consistency is one move, not a setting

AI gives you the average of the internet unless you hand it your taste. So today everyone captures their taste once as a Brand Book, and pastes it in before every request. Same brief in → on-brand out, every time. That kills “generic” and “inconsistent” in one go.

D

Website design system · 12 min

Don't invent components — theme a proven base. We use Material Design.

Material Design as the base → themed to your brand

A design system = your brand tokens (colour, type, spacing, shape) applied to a solid component base. Inventing buttons/cards/spacing from scratch is where slop creeps in. Material 3 (Google's system) gives you accessible colour roles, a type scale, 4/8px spacing, and real components for free — you just theme it so it looks like you, not default Material.

⚡ Faster start: design the screens in Google Stitch (it generates UI you can tweak) or describe them straight into Bolt — either way, feed it your tokens + Brand Block. And open Google AI Studio for free, generous image generation.

🎨 Colour
Material Theme Builder: drop your brand hex → full accessible scheme + tokens.
🔠 Type
Material type scale, set to your two Google Fonts.
📐 Spacing & grid
Material's 4/8px rhythm — instant hierarchy.
🧩 Components
Buttons, cards, nav — proven, accessible.
⬜ Shape
Your corner radius + accents = not “default Material”.
step 1 · Material Theme Builder (free)Open material-foundation.github.io/material-theme-builder → paste your brand primary colour → export the colour tokens.
step 2 · paste into Bolt / Lovable / v0“Build my website using Material 3 as the base design system, themed with these tokens: [paste tokens]. Heading font [X], body font [Y]. Sections: hero, about, services, proof, contact. Customise so it does NOT look default-Material: [corner radius], accent [#__], generous whitespace, my imagery style. Match the layout vibe of [reference]. [paste Brand Block]”

The Prompt Pack

Polished, ready-to-use prompts engineered for stunning, on-brand output — high contrast, distinctive type, zero slop. Fill the [brackets] and paste your Brand Block where noted.

1 · Brand strategy (AI interviews you)
📋 copy
You're a senior brand strategist. Interview me ONE question at a
time about what I do, who I serve, and how I want to feel — ask 5
sharp questions, waiting for each answer. Then give me: a one-line
positioning, a 3-word personality, a short mission, 5 taglines, and
"words we own / words we avoid". Specific and human — no corporate fluff.
2 · Colour palette + type (distinctive, accessible)
📋 copy
Design a visual identity for a brand that feels [3 words].
1) A 5-colour palette as hex — background, surface, text, primary,
   accent — each role labelled. Text on background must pass WCAG AA
   contrast. No purple-to-blue gradient.
2) A heading + body font pairing, free on Google Fonts, with real
   character. Do NOT use Inter, Instrument Sans, Roboto, Open Sans,
   Poppins, Montserrat or Lato. Prefer a display serif (Fraunces /
   Playfair Display / DM Serif Display) + a clean grotesk (Space
   Grotesk / Hanken Grotesk / Sora). Exact names + one line on why.
3 · Logo (distinctive, scalable)
📋 copy
Design a distinctive logo for "[name]", a [3 words] brand. Give 4
directions: a wordmark, a symbol, and a combined lockup. Must read at
16px and in one colour, high contrast, memorable, timeless — NOT a
generic rounded-gradient app blob, no clip-art. Brand colour [hex].
Show each on a light and a dark background.
4 · Brand sheet (your brand book, in HTML)
📋 copy
Build a one-page brand book as clean HTML: my logo, my colour palette
with hex + roles, a type specimen for my two fonts (headings + body),
sample buttons, and my voice in 3 words. Generous whitespace, strong
hierarchy, fonts loaded from Google Fonts. My brand: [paste Brand Block].
5 · Backgrounds & patterns
📋 copy
Create on-brand backgrounds for [brand] from my palette [hex], mood
[3 words]: (a) a soft gradient mesh, (b) a subtle geometric pattern,
(c) a clean solid with one accent shape. Keep contrast low enough for
text on top. Give 1920×1080 and 1080×1920. No busy stock textures,
no AI noise.
6 · Social post (stunning, on-brand)
📋 copy
Create a 1080×1080 Instagram post for [brand]. Headline: "[message]".
Use my exact colours [hex] and fonts [names]. Editorial and striking:
one bold focal point, generous negative space, strong type hierarchy,
high contrast, my voice [3 words]. No plastic AI sheen, no generic
stock, no clutter. [paste Brand Block]
7 · Poster / flyer (print-ready)
📋 copy
Design a one-page A4 poster for [event]. My brand: [paste Brand Block].
High-contrast and striking, clear hierarchy (eyebrow → headline →
details → CTA), lots of whitespace, my display font for the headline.
Then give a print-ready version (CMYK, 300 dpi, 3mm bleed) to save as PDF.
8 · Website (Material base, themed to you)
📋 copy
Build my website on Material 3 as the base, themed to my brand.
Tokens: [paste Material Theme Builder export]. Heading font [X], body
[Y]. Sections: hero, about, services, proof, contact. Premium and
editorial — NOT default Material, NOT generic AI SaaS: accent [hex],
[N]px corners, generous whitespace, real hierarchy, subtle hover/scroll
motion. Match the layout vibe of [reference]. High contrast, AA accessible.
[paste Brand Block]
9 · Steal a style with JSON (two steps)
📋 copy
STEP 1 (attach a reference image): Describe this image's STYLE as a
reusable JSON prompt — keys: style, lighting, palette, composition,
mood, camera, texture. Do NOT describe the subject.

STEP 2: Generate this JSON, changing ONLY "subject" to [your subject].
Keep everything else identical.
10 · Animate the logo
📋 copy
Animate my logo as an SVG: each stroke draws on in sequence, then a
gentle echo pulse (subtle scale). Smooth easing, ~1.5s, loops slowly,
my brand colours. Give me the HTML/SVG file.
11 · Presentation
📋 copy
From this outline [paste], create a [N]-slide deck. Apply my brand:
colours [hex], fonts [names], my voice. One idea per slide, strong
hierarchy, lots of whitespace — not template-y.
12 · Report / document
📋 copy
Draft a [length] report on [topic] from these notes: [...]. Structured
and plain, in my voice [3 words]. Cite only real, verifiable sources
with links. I'll fact-check before publishing.
13 · Correct it (when it's not right)
📋 copy
Here's a screenshot of your output. Change ONLY these, nothing else:
1) [exact change], 2) [exact change]. Keep everything else identical.
