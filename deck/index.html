Meet your intern

How AI actually thinks — just enough to direct it like a designer.

How the intern actually thinks

The world's most well-read autocomplete.

What people assume

It looks up the answer

Like searching a database and fetching a stored fact. It does not work this way.

What it really does

It predicts the next word

Having read a vast slice of the internet, it continues your sentence with the most likely next fragment — one at a time. Fluent, fast, occasionally confidently wrong.

Mechanism: large language models generate by next-token prediction — not retrieval.

A word you'll hear all day

A “token” is a fragment of text.

The intern reads and writes in puzzle-pieces — roughly 3–4 characters each. “Designing” might be design + ing. Everything it remembers and everything it costs is counted in tokens.

Images work differently

The sculptor in the marble.

Image AI (a “diffusion” model) starts with pure static and removes the noise step by step until your picture emerges — like chipping a figure out of stone.

Diffusion models denoise from random noise toward the prompt.

One more word

Multimodal = one intern, many senses.

Modern models can read your text, see an image you paste, and sometimes hear audio. So you can show the intern a reference — not only describe it.

The only jargon you need today

Eight words, sixty seconds.

01PromptThe brief you give the intern.
02TokenA fragment of text (~3–4 chars).
03Context windowHow much it holds in mind at once.
04ModelThe specific intern (GPT, Claude…).
05HallucinationA confident, fluent wrong answer.
06DiffusionHow image AI builds from noise.
07MultimodalReads text, sees images.
08System promptStanding rules it reads first.
Hold on to this
It's a pattern-completer, not a truth-engine.
Fluent does not mean correct.

Keep it sharp

Why your intern drifts mid-project — and the moves that fix it.

Why your intern sometimes loses the plot

The cluttered desk.

The “context window” is how much the intern can hold on its desk at once. Pile on a long, messy chat and papers slide off the edge. A longer chat is not a smarter chat.

It's documented, not a vibe

Lost in the middle.

Give a model a long input and it attends well to the beginning and end — but glazes over the middle. Accuracy can fall 30%+ when the key fact sits in the centre.

So: put your most important instruction first or last.

Liu et al., “Lost in the Middle,” TACL 2024.

The newest finding

Context rot is real.

A 2025 study tested 18 leading models. Every single one got less reliable as the input grew longer — even those advertising giant context windows. A bigger desk doesn't make a tired intern sharper.

Chroma Research, “Context Rot,” 2025.

Two ways the intern fibs

The guesser & the people-pleaser.

Hallucination

The confident exam-guesser

Never penalised for a wrong guess, so it always fills something in — dressed in fluent prose. Verify every fact, stat, dose, citation.

Sycophancy

The agreeable dinner guest

Trained on what people liked, so it tends to agree with you. Don't ask “I'm right, yeah?” — ask neutrally, and tell it to argue the opposite.

Kalai et al. (OpenAI, 2025) on hallucination; Sharma et al. (Anthropic, 2023) on sycophancy.

Un-dumb your intern

When it drifts — do this.

  • Fresh chat per task. New job? New conversation.
  • Be specific. Vague in, vague out.
  • Give examples of what “good” looks like.
  • Say what NOT to do.
  • Break big asks into steps.
  • Key info first or last — never buried.
  • Ask it to argue against you.
  • Carry your context over (see below).
Before leaving a long chat: “Summarise everything important from this chat so I can paste it into a fresh one.”

How AI hands you the finished thing

Two forms: pixels or code.

Pixels · image

An image generator paints it

GPT Image, Nano Banana, Midjourney, Ideogram. Fast and easy. Raster only, text can garble. Great for art, posts, posters.

A coding AI writes the real thing

The AI makes the finished design. (A tool like Canva is only for hand-collating if you don't code — that's not the AI part.)

The pro move for pictures

Stop writing paragraphs.
Send a spec.

Treat the model less like an artist you ramble at, and more like a design API. Name each ingredient and nothing gets lost.

  • Kills ambiguity — no “word salad”.
  • Consistent across generations.
  • Tweak one field, regenerate.
Quick one-off? A plain sentence is fine. JSON shines for precision, brand consistency & repeatability.
{
  "subject": "ceramic coffee cup, steam",
  "style": "editorial product photo",
  "composition": "top-down, negative space",
  "lighting": "soft morning window",
  "palette": ["#F2C6B4","#7A4F6B"],
  "mood": "calm, premium",
  "aspect_ratio": "1:1",
  "avoid": ["text","clutter"]
}
The killer workflow

Reference → JSON → restyle.

1 · Reference

Find an image whose look you love.

"style": "…",
"lighting": "…",
"palette": […],
"camera": "…"
2 · Extract JSON

“Describe this image as a JSON prompt I can reuse.”

3 · Restyle

Keep the style — change only the subject to yours. Generate.

It works because a multimodal model can see the reference and translate its look into fields you can edit.
Get it right

Image gen — dos & don'ts.

Do

  • Start from a reference; let AI write the JSON.
  • Change one field at a time.
  • Name lighting, camera & palette explicitly.
  • Put your brand HEX in the palette field.
  • Generate several, then choose.

Don't

  • Write a 500-word run-on paragraph.
  • Trust text inside images — fix the wording yourself.
  • Expect an exact hex — fine-tune colour after.
  • Copy a living artist's style.
  • Paste anything private into free tools.

The thing nobody tells you

Screen ≠ print.

Colour

Screens are RGB; print is CMYK. Convert, or colours shift. Lock exact brand hex in your file.

Resolution

Print needs 300 dpi plus bleed and safe margins so nothing gets cut off.

Export

Send a print-ready PDF. Re-typeset any AI text so it's crisp and correct.

Most AI tools are digital-first — check these three before you pay for a print run.

The buzzword that ties it together

What's a system prompt?

A normal prompt is one instruction inside the chat. A system prompt sits above it — standing rules the intern reads first, every single time, before anything you type.

System: “You are EkhoPost's designer. Always use our colours, fonts & warm, playful voice.”
You: “Make a post about our new feature.”

Step 1 · Strategy

Let the intern interview you.

Paste this and answer its questions one by one:

“You're a brand strategist. Interview me one question at a time about what I do, who I serve, and how I want to feel. After 5 questions, give me a one-line positioning, a 3-word personality, a mission, and 5 tagline options.”
  • Who I serve
  • What I stand for
  • Personality — 3 words
  • Mission + tagline
Step 2 · Inspiration & moodboard

Give the intern a moodboard.

Path A · gather first

Collect 5–8 references

Posters, palettes, brands you love — from Pinterest or your camera roll. Then show them to the AI: “Here's my vibe — suggest a direction.”

Path B · ask first

Let AI propose directions

“Based on my brand above, suggest 3 distinct visual directions — colours, mood, and a reference style for each.” Then pick one to refine.

Either way, the intern designs far better after it has seen your taste.
Step 3 · Visual identity

Give it a face.

Palette

“Give 5 HEX colours for a [3-word] brand, named, with where to use each.”

Type

“Suggest a heading + body font pairing, free on Google Fonts, that feels [personality].”

Logo

Generate a concept (Ideogram for words), then refine it.

Reality check: AI won't hit an exact hex — lock your final colours in your file or code.
Step 4 · Voice

How you sound.

Voice is your personality (constant). Tone is your mood (reads the room). Lock three trait words and a do/don't list.

My voice is…

warm · plain-spoken · quietly witty

Use: real, care, you, simple

Avoid: synergy, unleash, revolutionary

Step 5 · Make one real thing

Have AI generate it.

Give the AI your colours, type, voice and a reference, and have it make one branded piece — a social post or mini-poster — as an image, or as HTML you can keep. Your brand, alive, in minutes.

Step 6 · Reuse it forever

Give AI access to your brand.

The catch nobody mentions: AI has no memory of your brand unless you hand it over — every single time. So you bottle it once, then feed it back.

Tell it

Paste your brand block on top — or save it as a system prompt / Custom Instructions.

Show it

Give it your actual files — a Canva Brand Kit, references, your templates — so it matches exactly.

BRAND: [name]
FOR: [who I serve]
PERSONALITY: warm, plain, witty
COLOURS: #… #… #…
FONTS: [head] / [body]
VOICE: use real, care · avoid jargon
ALWAYS: short, human, specific

This is the “tell it” version — paste it before any request.

Two levels of reuse

A style note vs your whole design system.

Level 1 · everyone

The Brand Block

A short paragraph — colours, fonts, voice. Paste it into any chat. Free & universal, perfect for copy and quick visuals. Like handing your intern a one-page style note.

Level 2 · going pro

The Design System

Your real components as files — colours, type, buttons, templates in a folder. Give it to an AI that reads files (e.g. a coding agent) and it reproduces your brand exactly. Like handing over your whole portfolio to copy.

These EkhoPost pieces were made the Level-2 way — a design system in a folder, handed to an AI that learned it. Start at Level 1; graduate when you're making a lot.

Ninety seconds that matter

Use it responsibly.

  • To own it, add your hand. Purely AI images aren't copyrightable in the US.
  • Don't copy living artists or brands. Real lawsuits are live right now.
  • Never paste PII or patient data into consumer AI. Free outputs can be public.
  • Disclose & verify. Label AI where it matters; fact-check every claim.
