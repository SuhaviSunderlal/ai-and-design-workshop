Your Brand Block (= your personal system prompt)

Paste it on top of any prompt so the intern always knows who you are.

# Paste me before any design or writing request BRAND: [name] FOR: [who I serve, in a few words] PERSONALITY: [your 3 words] MISSION: [one line] COLOURS: #___ #___ #___ FONTS: [heading] / [body] VOICE: use [words we use] · avoid [words we avoid] ALWAYS: be specific, human, and brief. Ask me before assuming.
Free way to keep it

A saved note

Keep the block in your Notes app. Paste it on top of any prompt, in any tool. Works everywhere, costs nothing.

Paid way (optional)

Always-on memory

Custom Instructions / Projects / a custom GPT load it automatically — no pasting. Nice-to-have, not needed.

A “system prompt” is the standing instruction an AI reads before everything else. Written down, your brand becomes exactly that.

3

Prompt Cheat-Sheet

The two formulas behind every good result.

Words

Role · Task · Context · Constraints · Format · Examples

Example: “You're a witty curator (role). Write 3 Instagram captions (task) for a charcoal series on caregivers (context). Under 20 words, no hashtags, no clichés (constraints). Return a list (format). Tone like: ‘…’ (example).”

Pictures

Subject · Medium · Composition · Lighting · Colour · Mood · Style · Ratio

Example: “A single soft leaf curving into a heartbeat line (subject), flat editorial illustration (medium), centred with negative space (composition), sage green on cream (colour), calm and trustworthy (mood), square (ratio). No text.”

Write image prompts as a scene, not keyword salad. Change one thing at a time when you iterate. Show a reference image when you can.

Seven Ready-to-Use Recipes

Copy, paste your Brand Block above each, and go.

1 · Brand interview
“You're a brand strategist. Interview me one question at a time about what I do, who I serve, and how I want to feel. After 5 questions, give me a one-line positioning, a 3-word personality, a mission, and 5 tagline options.”
2 · Inspiration directions
“Based on my brand above, suggest 3 distinct visual directions — colours, mood, and a reference style for each — so I can pick one to build a moodboard around.”
3 · Colour palette
“Give me 5 HEX colours for a brand that feels [your 3 words]. Name each and tell me where to use it (background, text, accent). Make sure text colours are readable.”
4 · Font pairing
“Suggest a heading + body font pairing, both free on Google Fonts, that feels [your 3 words]. Explain why they work together.”
5 · Logo concept
“A minimal logo mark for [brand]: [one clear visual idea], [colour] on [background], flat editorial style, lots of negative space, square. No text.” (Use Ideogram if you want words in it.)
6 · Social post
“Write a [platform] post for [brand] about [topic], in my brand voice above. Under [N] words. Then suggest one image idea using the picture formula.”
7 · Rewrite in my voice
“Rewrite this in my brand voice above, keeping it warm and plain. Cut anything that sounds like jargon: [paste your draft].”
5

“Un-Dumb Your AI” Card

When the intern drifts, do these.

  • Fresh chat per task — new job, new conversation.
  • Be specific — vague in, vague out.
  • Give examples of what “good” looks like.
  • Say what NOT to do.
  • Break big asks into steps.
  • Key info first or last — never buried in the middle.
  • Ask it to argue against you — beats a yes-man.
  • Carry context over — see the move below.
The killer move: before you abandon a long chat, say — “Summarise everything important from this chat so I can paste it into a fresh one.” Then start a new chat with that summary.

Why this works: long inputs lose the middle (Liu et al., “Lost in the Middle,” 2024); all models degrade as context grows (Chroma, “Context Rot,” 2025); models guess confidently (OpenAI, 2025) and tend to agree with you (Anthropic, 2023).

7

Responsible-Use Card

Short, sourced, and genuinely matters.

  • To own it, add your hand. In the US, purely AI-generated images aren't copyrightable — a prompt-only logo can be freely copied. Edit and arrange it yourself to claim authorship. (US Copyright Office, 2025; Supreme Court declined to revisit, Mar 2026.)
  • Don't imitate living artists or brands. Avoid “in the style of [artist]” and recognisable characters/logos. (Disney v. Midjourney, 2025; Andersen v. Stability, trial Sept 2026.)
  • Never paste PII or patient data into a consumer AI tool — free outputs can be public, and policies vary.
  • Counter-steer bias. Image AI defaults to stereotypes; ask for diversity and review before publishing.
  • Disclose where it matters. Label AI in promotion (FTC); EU rules require machine-readable labels from Aug 2026 — “Content Credentials” (C2PA) is the standard.
  • Verify every fact. Doses, stats, citations — fluent doesn't mean correct.
